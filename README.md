Project Overview 

This project provides a detailed analysis of medical equipment maintenance data to identify cost drivers, failure patterns, and areas for strategic intervention. The analysis leverages a dataset covering several years of maintenance records, device specifications, and geographical locations.

*Tools:* Tableau (Visualization), Excel/CSV (Data Cleaning and Preparation).

## ðŸ”‘ Key Findings and Insights 

The analysis highlighted several critical areas:

### A. Performance Over Time and Device Age

* *Cost Peak:* The year *2018* recorded the highest maintenance cost and failure count.
* *Device Age Risk:* Maintenance cost increases significantly with device age, reaching its peak between *6 and 9 years*. (New equipment, age 1, predictably has very low costs).
* *Downtime:* Complex failures, though less frequent, consume the *most downtime* to repair (High maintenance class tasks take an average of 16 hours).

### B. Equipment and Manufacturer Focus

* *High-Cost Devices:* *X-Ray Machines, Defibrillators, and PET Scanners* are the most expensive devices to maintain.
* *Costly Models:* Models *500, 350, and 150* showed the highest maintenance costs.
* *Manufacturers:* *ScanPro, ImaginTech, and RescueTech* lead in total maintenance costs. *ScanPro, Medeqube, and ImaginTech* have the highest failure counts.

### C. Geographical Impact

* *Japan* has the highest total maintenance cost among all countries analyzed.
